All about dinosaurs

The first dinosaurs on earth appeared around 250 million years ago during the Triassic Period. Over millions of years, lots of new and exciting types of dinosaurs evolved. From the tiny, Compsognathus which was no bigger than a chicken to huge giants such as Argentinosaurus, which was as heavy as six fire engines. The dinosaurs thrived on earth for more than 185 million years, until their extinction at the end of the Cretaceous period 65 million years ago.

What are dinosaurs?

Dinosaurs are ancient, prehistoric reptiles that lived during the Mesozoic Era. Some dinosaurs had long necks for grazing treetops, some had fearsome claws and sharp teeth for catching prey, some even had huge spikes and bony, armoured bodies for fending off preators. They had bony skeletons, leathery skin and, in some cases, even feathers. Some dinosaurs ate only leaves & ferns which we call herbivores. Some dinosaurs were meat eaters and hunted other dinosaurs for dinner. These are called carnivores. The dinosaurs came in all shapes and sizes. Microraptor, for example was the size of a turkey, whereas Spinosaurus was taller than a two-storey house and longer than a double-decker bus. How do we know about dinosaurs?

Scientists who study dinosaurs are called palaeontologists. Palaeontologists dig fossils to learn about life in prehistoric times. Fossils are the remains of animals or plants, or dinosaurs that died many thousands or millions of years ago. Fossils form when the dinosaur, for example, dies and its body falls into sand or mud. Over time more and more layers of sand and mud build up and eventually turns the bones to rock. By studying these bones scientists can tell all about how a dinosaur lived, how big it was and even what kind of food it ate.

What happened to the dinosaurs?

Dinosaurs went extinct around 65 million years ago. At that time a huge asteroid crashed into the earth at Yucatan Peninsula in Mexico. The impact caused huge amounts of dust and ash to go uo into the atmosphere and block out the suns light and heat. It caused the planets climate to change dramatically. During the same period there were lots of volcanic eruptions happening that were sending clouds of gas into the atmosphere which would have added to climate change. Nearly 90% of all species including the dinosaurs went extinct at this time.
