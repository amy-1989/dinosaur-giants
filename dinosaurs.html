The largest creatures that ever lived

Carnivores

Meat-eating dinosaurs

Spinosaurus

The first Spinosaurus fossil was destroyed during World War II. The Spinosaurus is the largest meat-eating land animal ever discovered. He was 50 feet long and as tall as a 2-storey house. His skull alone was a whopping 6ft long, and he had a huge, distinctive sail fin on his back. He fed on a diet of fish and other dinosaurs. He roamed the floodplains of what is now North Africa, during the Late Cretaceous period.

Giganotosaurus

Giganotosaurus is one of the largest predators known to have existed. The first Giganotosaurus fossil was of a leg bone & discovered in 1993. Giganotosaurus thin, razorlike teeth measured 6inchs and its head was a gigantic 6.5 ft long, his body was 40ft long, and he weighed 8000kg or approx 125 people. It fed on other large dinosaurs and animals. Giganotosaurus roamed the plains of South America from the Early Cretaceous period.

Tyrannosaurus Rex

Tyrannosaurus Rex is probably the most famous of all the dinosaurs. Its name means King of the Lizards. It was thought to be the largest carnivore until recent Spinosaurus fossil finds found that Spinosaurus grew to be a bigger size. T-rex had a massive 6ft long skull, with banana shaped teeth, 20cm long.Its bite was strong enough to crush a car. T-rex was 40ft in length,weighed twice as much as an elephant and had feathers. Despite its whopping size it had incredibly small arms. It fed on a diet of meat, hunting other dinosaurs. Tyrannosaurus roamed the lands of North America during the Late Cretaceous period.

Herbivores

Plant-eating dinosaurs

Argentinosaurus

Argentinosaurus was a gigantic dinosaur. A complete skeleton of this dinosaur has not yet been discovered. Scientists estimate it would have weighed approx 100tons or the same as 6 fire engines. It had huge pillarlike legs to support its weight. It fed on plant matter and would have had to eat approx 230kg of leaves a day. It was 115ft in length, and is the largest land animal ever to walk the earth. It did not stop growing during its lifetime. Argentinosaurus would have roamed what is now Argentina during the Late Cretaceous.

Mamenchisaurus

Mamenchisaurus had a very long neck and a tiny head. It fed on a diet of plants and leaves. Mamenchisaurus has the longest neck of any dinosaur at a giant 50ft long. They lived in family groups. It was a massive 85ft long. It roamed the plains of what is now China during the Late Jurassic period.

Brachiosaurus

Brachiosaurus is one of the most famous herbivore dinosaurs. It had a long neck and pillar like legs. Its front legs were longer than its hind legs. It was 80ft long and almost 50ft tall. Its fossils have been found in North America, Europe and Africa. It roamed the forests of the Late Jurassic period.
